Questions

Frequently asked.

Straight answers about what OpenHelix is, what it can do, and what to watch for.

What is OpenHelix?+
OpenHelix is a Web3 launch toolkit. From one dashboard you can create tokens, lock liquidity, set up vesting, and verify contracts — without writing code. It's the on-ramp for turning an idea into a real on-chain project.
Which blockchains are supported?+
Token creation works across BNB Chain, Ethereum, Base, Arbitrum, Optimism, Polygon and Avalanche (each with a free testnet and mainnet), plus Solana. You pick the network from the dropdown in the Studio.
Do I need to know how to code?+
No. You fill in a short form — name, symbol, supply — and your wallet does the rest. The Studio builds and sends the transaction for you.
Does OpenHelix hold my funds or private keys?+
Never. Every action is signed in your own wallet (MetaMask for EVM chains, Phantom for Solana). OpenHelix has no access to your keys and never takes custody of your funds.
Is it safe to use on mainnet?+
Token creation is a standard, well-understood operation and is fine on mainnet. The fund-holding features (liquidity locking and vesting) are kept testnet-only until they've had a professional security audit — they hold real value, so we don't rush them onto mainnet. Always do a free testnet run first.
What does it cost?+
OpenHelix itself is the tooling — you only pay the blockchain's own network (gas) fees, which go to the network, not to us. On testnets those fees are free using faucet funds, so you can practice the whole flow at no cost.
Can I try it for free first?+
Yes — start on any testnet (BNB Testnet, Sepolia, Solana Devnet, etc.). Grab free test funds from that network's faucet, create a token, and confirm everything works before spending anything real.
Is this financial or legal advice?+
No. OpenHelix is software tooling. Launching tokens and raising funds can carry legal and tax obligations that vary by country — talk to a qualified lawyer and accountant before doing anything involving real money or public fundraising.
